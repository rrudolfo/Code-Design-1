Bill Russell

Bill Russell was one of the greatest champions in sports history, known for his leadership, defense, and unmatched drive to win as a player and a coach. Russell played for the University of San Francisco Dons for two years before being drafted 2nd overall by the Boston Celtics in 1956. He played nine seasons before becoming a player coach in 1966. During his time he won 11 NBA championships.

    All About Mr. 11 Rings

  • 1934 - Born in Monroe, Louisiana
  • 1953 - Starts college career for the University of San Francisco Dons
  • 1956 - Drafted 2nd overall by the Boston Celtics
  • 1957 - Wins first NBA championship with the Boston Celtics
  • 1966 - Celtics win eighth consecutive NBA championships
  • 1966 - Becomes player-coach for the Boston Celtics
  • 1968 - Wins 11th NBA championship, his final one as a player
  • 1969 - Retires as a player
  • 1975 - Begins coaching career
  • 1988 - Officially retires from the game of basketball
  • 2022 - Passed away at the age of 88
